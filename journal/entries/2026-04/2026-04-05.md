### Summary
The developer created PRD #51, a specification for upgrading the `/research` skill to persist research output across sessions and enable reuse of prior findings.

The core problem the developer identified was that research runs produce valuable but ephemeral output—once a conversation ends, that work disappears. There's no way to know what's already been researched, avoid duplicate work, or build incrementally on prior findings. Running research on the same topic twice wastes time and money without compounding value.

The solution involves writing research output to `docs/research/<topic-slug>.md` within each project repo, maintaining a lightweight index at `docs/research/index.md` to track what's been researched, checking that index before starting new research, and updating existing files rather than creating duplicates. The developer made seven design decisions to support this: storing research per-project (not globally), using topic slugs without date prefixes in filenames, maintaining a one-line-per-file index table, updating existing files instead of creating dated copies, requiring a mandatory changelog entry for every update documenting what was removed and why, defining a consistent research file format with date header and update log, and having the skill scan open PRDs after saving to add cross-references where relevant.
