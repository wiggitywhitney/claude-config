The developer added a handoff readiness check to the prd-update-progress skill and expanded the global CLAUDE.md rules to cover progress tracking and roadmap lifecycle management.

The developer wanted to address a recurring friction point: when the prd-update-progress skill suggests running `/clear` to wipe context, the next AI instance often lacks critical information to continue work. The developer proposed adding an explicit step where the implementing AI answers the question "will the next instance have everything it needs to pick up where I left off?" before suggesting context be cleared. If the answer is no, the AI should fix the gaps—documenting decisions, updating PROGRESS.md, capturing open questions, clarifying the next task's entry point, and recording workarounds or gotchas—rather than moving forward with an incomplete handoff.

The developer updated both versions of the prd-update-progress skill (the careful and yolo variants) to include this new Step 8.9, which walks through those five specific checks explicitly and instructs the AI not to suggest `/clear` until all answers are genuinely yes.

The developer also expanded CLAUDE.md to formalize how PROGRESS.md and ROADMAP.md should be maintained. PROGRESS.md entries follow Keep a Changelog style—dated, prose-driven, explaining what changed and why—and exclude internal metadata like issue numbers or milestone IDs. ROADMAP.md gets updated on PRD lifecycle events: entries are added when a PRD is created and removed when it closes, keeping the roadmap forward-looking while completed work lives in the changelog. The developer also extended the `/write-prompt` trigger to cover PRDs since they function as prompts that future agents read and act on.
